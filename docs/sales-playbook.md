# InRange Real Estate — Sales Force Playbook
## 2 Teams · 30 Agents · NY & NJ Markets

---

## 1. Team Structure

Each market (NY, NJ) runs as an independent unit under a **Team Leader** who reports to ownership.

```
Owner / Principal Broker
├── NY Team Leader (1)
│   ├── Senior Agents — Residential Buyers/Sellers (4)
│   ├── Senior Agents — Multi-Family & New Development (3)
│   ├── Rental / Leasing Agents (3)
│   ├── Inside Sales Associates / ISAs (3)
│   └── Transaction Coordinator (1)
│
└── NJ Team Leader (1)
    ├── Senior Agents — Residential Buyers/Sellers (4)
    ├── Senior Agents — Multi-Family & New Development (3)
    ├── Rental / Leasing Agents (3)
    ├── Inside Sales Associates / ISAs (3)
    └── Transaction Coordinator (1)
```

### Role Definitions

| Role | Count/Team | Primary Responsibility |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| Team Leader | 1 | Pipeline oversight, coaching, recruiting, culture |
| Sr. Residential Agent | 4 | Buyer and seller representation, listings |
| Sr. Multi-Family / Dev Agent | 3 | Building sales, new development, investor clients |
| Rental / Leasing Agent | 3 | Tenant placement, landlord relationships, renewals |
| ISA (Inside Sales Associate) | 3 | Inbound/outbound lead qualification, appointment setting |
| Transaction Coordinator | 1 | Contract-to-close admin, compliance, paperwork |

### Hiring Mix (Hybrid Approach)
- **Retain**: High performers with 6+ closed deals in past 12 months move into Senior roles
- **Promote**: Existing junior agents with potential → ISA → Leasing → Senior track
- **Recruit**: Fill ISA and Leasing roles externally first; source Senior agents via referral and LinkedIn

## 2. Compensation Plan

### Team Leader
- Base salary: $80,000–$100,000/yr
- Override: 0.5% on every team transaction (paid monthly)
- Quarterly bonus: $5,000 if team hits volume target
- Annual retention bonus: $10,000 if team retains 12+ of 15 members

### Senior Residential & Multi-Family Agents

| GCI Tier (Annual) | Agent Split | Company Split |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------|
| $0 – $60K | 60% | 40% |
| $60K – $120K | 70% | 30% |
| $120K – $200K | 75% | 25% |
| $200K+ | 80% | 20% |

- Splits reset each January 1
- Agents pay E&O, MLS dues, marketing costs from their split

### Rental / Leasing Agents
- Base: $36,000/yr ($3,000/mo)
- Commission: 25% of one month's rent per lease signed
- Landlord retainer bonus: $500 per exclusive landlord account secured
- No split tiers — flat commission on top of base

### ISAs
- Base: $42,000/yr ($3,500/mo)
- Per qualified appointment set (shown): $75
- Per appointment that converts to signed contract: $250 additional
- Monthly cap on bonuses: $2,000 (to be revisited at 6-month review)

### Transaction Coordinator
- Salary: $52,000–$62,000/yr
- Per-file bonus: $75 per closed transaction above 8 closings/month

## 3. Lead Generation System

### Source Mix by Division

#### Residential (Buyers & Sellers)
| Source | Owner | Goal/Month |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Zillow / Realtor.com Premier | Team + ISAs | 40 leads |
| Google Ads (local market) | Company-paid | 25 leads |
| Sphere of influence / referral | Each agent (5/agent) | 20 leads |
| Open houses | Listing agents | 15 walk-ins |
| Social media (Instagram, FB) | Team Leader | 15 leads |
| Past client database (email/SMS) | ISAs | 20 re-engagements |

#### Multi-Family & New Development
| Source | Owner | Goal/Month |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| CoStar / LoopNet | Sr. MF agents | 10 investor leads |
| Developer direct outreach | Sr. Dev agents | 5 new projects/contacts |
| Investor meetups / REI clubs | Team Leader + agents | 8 connections |
| LinkedIn outreach | ISAs | 15 investor touches |
| Attorney & CPA referral network | Team Leader | 5 referrals |

#### Rentals / Leasing
| Source | Owner | Goal/Month |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| StreetEasy (NY) / Zillow (NJ) | Leasing agents | 50 renter inquiries |
| Landlord cold outreach | ISAs | 20 landlord contacts |
| Property management partnerships | Team Leader | 3 PM firm relationships |
| Current tenant referral incentive | Leasing agents | 10 referrals |

### Lead Routing Rules
1. All inbound web/ad leads → ISA within 5 minutes (speed-to-lead SLA)
2. ISA qualifies using BANT: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline
3. Qualified buyer/seller → assigned to Sr. Residential agent by territory zip code
4. Investor / building inquiry → Sr. MF agent
5. Rental inquiry → Leasing agent
6. Unqualified but not dead → ISA nurture sequence (60-day email/SMS drip)

### CRM Workflow
- Platform: Follow Up Boss (recommended) or Salesforce (enterprise)
- Every lead entered within 1 hour of contact
- 5-touch sequence within first 7 days: call → text → email → call → voicemail
- Pipeline stages: New → Contacted → Qualified → Under Contract → Closed / Lost

## 5. Motivation & Retention System

### Recognition Framework

#### Daily
- Team chat (Slack / GroupMe) "Win of the Day" post — anyone shares a win; Team Leader acknowledges every one publicly

#### Weekly
- **#1 Dialer Award**: ISA with most dials gets $50 gift card on Friday
- **Deal Board**: Physical whiteboard in office with all active deals + agent names visible to everyone

#### Monthly
- **Closer of the Month**: Highest GCI agent gets reserved parking, name on board, $500 bonus
- **Leasing MVP**: Most leases signed — $300 bonus + lunch with Team Leader
- **ISA of the Month**: Most qualified appointments converted — $300 bonus
- Team lunch if collective monthly GCI target is hit

#### Quarterly
- **Top Performer Trip**: #1 agent by GCI per quarter earns 1 airline ticket ($500 credit) toward a trip
- Team dinner (company-paid) if team hits 110%+ of quarterly GCI target
- Promotion reviews: ISA → Leasing → Junior Sr. → Sr. agent track reviewed quarterly

#### Annual
- Presidents Club: Top 2 agents per team (4 total) + team leaders attend a company retreat / experience
- Annual awards ceremony with full company, spouses invited
- Equity / partnership track conversation for agents at $300K+ GCI for 2+ consecutive years

### Culture Practices

**Onboarding (First 30 Days)**
- Week 1: Shadow a Senior agent on 3 appointments minimum
- Week 2: Role-play scripts with Team Leader daily (15 min)
- Week 3: First solo appointments with Team Leader on standby
- Week 4: First solo deal or lease targeted; debrief with team

**Ongoing Training**
- Monday 8:30 AM: Weekly team huddle — market update, wins, deal blocks, motivation (30 min max, never runs over)
- Bi-weekly: Script practice / objection handling drill (30 min, rotating facilitator)
- Monthly: Guest speaker — top agent, attorney, lender, developer
- Quarterly: Off-site half-day training (negotiation, market analysis, tech tools)

**Accountability Without Micromanagement**
- Agents own their pipeline; Team Leader coaches, doesn't manage every call
- If agent misses weekly minimums 2 weeks in a row → private 1:1 reset conversation
- 3rd miss in a rolling 60-day period → written Performance Improvement Plan (30-day)
- PIP failure → exit conversation

**Team Identity**
- Each team names itself (NY and NJ separately) — builds ownership and pride
- Team jerseys / branded gear for each market
- Healthy inter-team competition: NY vs NJ leaderboard on shared Slack channel (opt-in)
- Team Leader has a $500/quarter "culture budget" — use it however the team decides

## 6. 90-Day Launch Plan

### Month 1 — Foundation
- [ ] Finalize team roster: identify retains, promotions, new hires to fill gaps
- [ ] Set up CRM, assign leads, build pipeline stages
- [ ] Hold individual comp plan conversations with each team member
- [ ] Launch ISA team with dedicated lead sources and scripts
- [ ] First Monday huddle cadence begins

### Month 2 — Activation
- [ ] All lead sources live and feeding CRM
- [ ] First full monthly scorecard reviewed with each team
- [ ] First Closer of the Month and ISA of the Month awards given
- [ ] Team Leader 1:1s weekly with every agent — identify early stars and at-risk
- [ ] First inter-team NY vs NJ leaderboard published

### Month 3 — Optimization
- [ ] Review lead source ROI — cut or double down based on conversion data
- [ ] First quarterly performance reviews — promotions and PIPs as needed
- [ ] Quarterly off-site training session
- [ ] Adjust targets based on first 60 days of data
- [ ] Recruit to backfill any exits; target pipeline of 3 candidates per open role at all times
